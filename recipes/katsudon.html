Recipe #3: Katsudon

Home Page

Katsudon is a delicious deep-fired and breaded cutlet meal that I grew most fond of during my time in Japan. It originates to the Meiji period and may have been invented in 1921. The dish itself is simple yet elegant in its umami flavors. Lets get started!

Ingredients

  • Tonkatsu
  • White Onion
  • Dashi (Broth of soup stock, mirin, and soy sauce)
  • Eggs
  • Green Onion
  • Japanese White Rice

Instructions

  1. Make Tonkatsu.
  2. Cook White Onion (diced) in savory Dashi broth.
  3. Place Tonkatsu on top to let it absorb the flavors of the broth.
  4. Add the beated Egg> mixture and cook till just set.
  5. Serve it all over steamed White Rice.

And that's pretty much all there is to it!!!
