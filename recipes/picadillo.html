Recipe #1: Picadillo

Home Page

Picadillo is one of my favorite dishes from the Puerto Rican tradition of culinary delights. The name originates from the Spanish word "Picar", or to mince. I typically use leftover taco meat to make this, but if you want to create a superb picadillo from scratch, this is how you do it!

Ingredients

  • Olive Oil
  • Onion (diced)
  • Red/Green Bell Pepper (diced)
  • Sofrito
  • Ground Beef (lean)
  • Sazon
  • Adobo
  • Bay Leaves
  • Tomato Sauce (Can)
  • Pimientos
  • Salt
  • Cilantro

Instructions

  1. Dice up veggies and cook with olive oil on medium heat for a few minutes.
  2. Add sofrito and saute for a few more minutes.
  3. Add the Ground Beef and break up with spoon as it browns.
  4. Add Sazon, Adobo, Bay Leaves, and Tomato Sauce and mix evenly. Add a dahs of water to aid in the stirring and let boil.
  5. Once boiling, lower temperature to a nice simmer for 10 minutes.
  6. When the meat is fully done, serve with a side of White Rice.

And that's pretty much all there is to it!!!
